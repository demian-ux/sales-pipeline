# Outreach context — Oaki Studio

> This file is the brief for any AI agent or collaborator running outreach
> work for oaki.studio. Read it once at the start of a session; refer back
> when scoring a signal, drafting a message, or deciding whether to act.
>
> The live `Campaigns` Google Sheet is the source of truth for campaign
> records. This doc captures the *strategy* behind them — the why, the
> audience, the tone — which the sheet alone does not convey.

---

## What oaki sells

High-end architectural visualization — editorial-quality rendering for
architects, real estate developers, interior designers, and competition
teams. The work is known for atmosphere, restraint, and premium craft.
Oaki is selective; not every project is a fit.

Founder: Demian Oki, Buenos Aires.

---

## ICP — who we want to work with

A potential client is a fit when they meet most of these:

- **Discipline:** architecture studio, interior design studio, real estate
  developer (luxury), or competition team.
- **Project type:** luxury residential, luxury hospitality, cultural,
  mixed-use, large competitions, infrastructure with civic gravitas.
- **Geography (current focus):** New York, Miami, France. Other cities
  on a case-by-case basis when the project warrants it.
- **Aesthetic match:** their visual identity already leans editorial,
  premium, materially restrained. We don't try to convert studios who
  ship generic CGI — we partner with studios whose taste maps to ours.
- **Scale:** mid-to-large projects. One-off small residentials usually
  aren't worth the relationship overhead.
- **Market position:** premium or ultra-premium. They sell at a level
  where editorial visuals change the outcome — sales, awards, investment.

**Red flag:** the only reason they'd buy is price. Oaki competes on
quality and narrative, not budget.

## What counts as an "interesting opportunity"

We act on real signals, never on cadence. A signal is interesting when it
gives us a *specific, defensible "why now."*

### Strong signals — worth a personalised touch

- **New project announcement or award win** — we can offer visuals that
  match that level of ambition.
- **Competition entry or deadline coming up** — visuals are often the
  deciding factor in jury reads.
- **Public event, gala, launch, exhibition** — natural conversation hook.
- **Hiring signal** — new marketing lead, design director, head of
  business development. They are inheriting a visual problem.
- **Visible gap** between their positioning ("we are the premium studio
  in X") and their current renders or website imagery.
- **A past project of theirs is entering awards season.**
- **A referral or warm intro** from someone in the network.
- **A lapsed relationship returning to a natural moment** — anniversary
  of a delivered project, their new launch, a market shift.
- **A pain point oaki uniquely solves:** editorial atmosphere, narrative
  coherence, competition-grade visuals, fast turnaround for high-stakes
  deliverables.

### Weak signals — flag honestly, do not act

- "Haven't heard from them in a while."
- Generic "checking in" with no specific hook.
- Sequence or cadence timing alone ("it's been 30 days").
- No specific research backing the "why now" — only intuition.
- A signal we cannot verify with a public source.

If a contact only has weak signals, the right answer is to wait, do more
research, or move on. **We do not send cold mass outreach.**

### How to read the scoring (1–10 scales, in the app)

- **business_fit_score** — fit with ICP.
- **taste_score** — visual / aesthetic alignment with oaki.
- **relationship_score** — depth of existing relationship.
- **opportunity_score** — strength of the current commercial moment;
  a real "why now" signal pushes this up.
- **priority_score** — overall strategic priority, factoring all the
  above.

High priority typically means a 7+ on `opportunity_score` **and**
`taste_score`. A 10/10 fit with `relationship_score` of 1 is still a
slow-build — not a fast ask.

## The four campaigns

All four are currently **Active**. Owner: Demian. Each is a distinct
lane with its own rationale, audience, and playbook — do not mix them.

### 1. Anchor Clients — `camp_001`

| | |
|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Quarterly strategic check-ins with top active clients — deepen the relationship and surface new pipeline. |
| **Audience** | Existing top clients sending consistent work (e.g. Côte Développement). |
| **Geography** | Miami, New York, France. |
| **Project types** | Luxury residential, luxury hospitality, competitions. |
| **Channel** | Email. |
| **Cadence** | Quarterly. |
| **CTA** | Set a discovery meeting. |

**Tone:** trust-building, not a sales push. Lead with curiosity about
their pipeline and a relevant share from oaki's recent work. Reference
specific past projects we delivered together.

**Watch for:** quarterly check-in cycle drifting. If the last meaningful
touch is more than ~90 days old for an anchor, that's the signal.

### 2. Past Events Leads — `camp_002`

| | |
|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Reconnect with leads met in person at 2026 events while the relationship is still warm. |
| **Audience** | Anyone Demian met at a gala, conference, or industry event in 2026 (e.g. AIANY Gala contacts). |
| **Channels** | Email + LinkedIn. |
| **Cadence** | Weekly. |
| **CTA** | Set a discovery meeting. |

**Tone:** personal, memory-anchored. Always reference the specific event
and what was discussed. The conversation is the hook — the studio
introduction is secondary.

**Never:** send a generic "great to meet you." If we can't remember
something specific, the lead is not ready for a touch yet.

### 3. Past Clients Rekindling — `camp_003`

| | |
|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Re-engage past clients who stopped sending work. |
| **Audience** | Past clients in New York and Miami, primarily in luxury residential, who went quiet (e.g. Maison Peralta). |
| **Offer** | Oaki's upgraded capabilities and refreshed portfolio. |
| **Known pain point** | Pricing was a concern previously. |
| **Channel** | Email. |
| **Cadence** | Weekly. |
| **CTA** | Set a discovery meeting. |

**Tone:** confident and visual. Lead with new work that demonstrates the
upgrade in quality and capability. Address pricing **indirectly by
showing value** — never lead with a discount or a defensive note.

**Frame:** they already know oaki. The job is to re-earn the project,
not re-introduce the studio.

### 4. Outreach Campaign — `camp_004`

| | |
|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Cold outreach to high-fit prospects in New York. |
| **Audience** | Architects, interior designers, and real estate developers in New York working on luxury residential or hospitality. |
| **Offer** | A visual narrative for architectural projects with an editorial look — winning work, investment, and attention. |
| **Pain point** | They need to excel in a competitive global market; better visuals are a lever. |
| **Channels** | Letter, email, LinkedIn. |
| **Cadence** | Twice weekly — **Tuesdays and Thursdays only**. |
| **CTA** | Set a discovery meeting. |

**Tone:** research-first. Only contact when there is a real, documented
signal (see *Strong signals* above). Open with the specific signal, not
with oaki. The first paragraph should be about *them*.

**Frame:** the slowest channel — and the highest-quality net-new
pipeline source. Quality beats volume. A week of well-researched
touches outperforms a hundred generic emails.

**No mass outreach.** If you can't write a specific "why now" for a
contact, they are not ready for this campaign.

## How to write any oaki message

These rules apply to every campaign and every channel.

- **Length:** 4–7 sentences. Letters can be slightly longer; LinkedIn
  notes shorter.
- **Voice:** calm, confident, premium. Written by a person, not a
  system.
- **Specific:** reference something real — a project, an event, an
  article, a recent hire, a competition deadline.
- **Subject lines:** concrete and specific. Never clickbait. Never
  "Quick question."
- **Forbidden openers:** "Hope you're well," "Hope this finds you well,"
  "Just checking in," "Touching base," "I wanted to reach out."
- **Forbidden devices:** fake urgency, pushy CTAs, exclamation marks,
  emoji, "circle back" language, generic compliments.
- **CTA:** singular, low-friction, specific. Default form:
  - "If useful, happy to send a short walkthrough."
  - "If the timing fits, a 20-minute call to compare notes."

---

## Hard product rules — never violate

- **No automation.** Every draft goes through Demian before it sends.
  AI generates copy; humans send it.
- **No mass outreach.** Generators produce one message at a time, tied
  to one specific signal. Never batched, never templated to a list.
- **Human-approved.** No AI output acts on Demian's behalf without an
  explicit click.
- **LinkedIn fields are tracking, not API hooks.** We do not log into
  LinkedIn through this app. The LinkedIn columns track manual touches.
- **Discovery ≠ Opportunity.** A *Discovery* is a market signal from an
  article. An *Opportunity* is a deal in motion attached to a Lead. A
  Discovery becomes an Opportunity only when a real lead is attached.

## Where this lives in the app

- The four campaigns are records in the `Campaigns` Google Sheet, read
  by `src/lib/sheets/campaigns.ts`.
- Brand voice for AI copy is in `src/lib/prompts/brand.ts` (the
  `BRAND_VOICE` constant). Every outreach prompt uses it as the system
  message.
- Lead-to-campaign assignment is the `campaign_id` field on each lead,
  set via bulk-assign in `/relationships` or per-lead in `/leads/[id]`.
- Signal extraction from articles lives in `src/lib/prompts/discoveries/`
  and `src/lib/prompts/research/`.
